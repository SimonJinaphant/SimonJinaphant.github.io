Microsoft | Summer Internship 2016

Hearing AI Team photo at Microsoft Redmond campus.


During the summer before second year I began polishing my portfolio of small projects, hoping to acquire at-least one internship offer for the summer of 2016; never did I imagine that I would end up being apart of Microsoft Vancouver as a software engineering intern, especially for my first ever software internship!

The Garage

The specific internship program I was apart of was Garage, which focuses on delivering large impact from small projects developed and designed from the ground up.

For the first week of the internship we were pitched various projects from different divisions/groups of Microsoft. Each project focused on different customers, technologies, and value; from building intelligent web bots to pioneering accessibility apps to help people access more of the world around them.

Hearing AI

My team focused on building an application which pioneers the concept of visualization and translation of sound for the deaf and hard of hearing community.

The origins of this project branched off of another Microsoft hackathon project from 2015, when a woman with hearing loss was unaware that her carbon monoxide detector was ringing for almost 2 weeks; putting her in a dangerous situation.

For ordinary people, sound is something taken for granted each day; sound provides contextual information -such as a sharp beeping noise provides an indication of distress in the environment- but this important information is not accessible to everyone.

Our project focused on the question “Why must sound only be heard?”. Through user research we learned that people who are deaf and hard of hearing rely a lot more on what they see. Our project aimed to compliment their prefered sense by translating the sound environment through visualization and transcription of speech -with a unique twist.

The Experience

For my first software engineering internship this program has been both challenging and rewarding. I joined this internship program with one goal in mind: To work with a community of people and build upon ideas together; and it was definitely met. Despite being the only intern out of cohort who never had any previous software development experience, I still felt welcome to the community.

My top contributions to the project includes:

  • Increased team productivity with an automated distribution system used to deliver daily updates and improvements to over 45 early adopters.
  • Established telemetry to gain insight on how customers use the app and how to improve their experience, leading to a weekly retention rate of 67%.
  • Implemented a web service that registers and grants access to other services without jeopardizing private data on both communication endpoints.
