I LOVE TO CONTRIBUTE IN TEAMS

UBC SNOWBOTS

MEMBER SINCE SEPTEMBER 2014

I am a proud member of UBC Snowbots, a student team dedicated to the research and development of autonomous ground vehicles. I was one of eight members selected to attend competition, where we ranked 4th in design and 5th in networking, against 40 other registered teams from around the world.

I am currently leading the design for the pathfinding system; this system allows the vehicle to compute the shortest route in order to autonomously navigate a 200 ft by 100 ft obstacle course.

My past accomplishments included optimizing the camera capturing software to increase performance by 4x in best case scenarios, along with resolving memory leaks and serial connection issues to increase productivity time by 20%.

Technologies used: C++, ROS, OpenCV, Linux, Git
